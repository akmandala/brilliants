FAQ — Snippets, selection, and libraries.

If you don’t see your question here, send a note — we’ll respond with the practical answer.

Generate free BoM + design notes

What’s included in the free automated output?

A real-part BoM list selected from our Snippet Database, plus a short design instruction paragraph describing wiring intent and key assumptions.

What’s NOT included in the free output?

Supplier-formatted BoMs (e.g., LCSC), part-library creation, and full schematic/PCB delivery — those are paid services.

Is the free tool limited?

Yes. Free auto-generation runs on a daily quota ({DAILY_LIMIT} sketches/day) to keep the service responsive.

Can I start with just a sketch?

Yes. A sketch + constraints is enough to generate a Snippet Pack.

Does the LLM generate circuits?

No. Snippets are stored in our database. The LLM only interprets your sketch/spec and selects the best-fit Snippets. Nothing is generated on the fly.

Can you select an MCU or processor Snippet too?

Yes. We infer requirements (interfaces, camera needs, AI/DSP workload, memory, power envelope, OS/software constraints) and rank compute Snippets from the database. The selection includes companion blocks like PMIC rails, clocks, boot storage, and interface protection.

Do you do simulation and derating?

Where it matters. Snippets include assumptions, constraints, and calculation/derating notes; simulation hooks are included when applicable.

Where do the symbols/footprints come from?

You can build your collection from sources such as SnapEDA, Ultra Librarian, and SamacSys, or commission a tailored Altium library built to IPC/house standards.

What files will we get?

Typically: Snippet Pack documentation, BoM exports, and part-library outputs (links or delivered libraries). If requested: Altium project structures and manufacturing-ready exports.

Can you keep our design confidential?

Yes. Share only what’s necessary; we can work with high-level specs and interfaces first.
